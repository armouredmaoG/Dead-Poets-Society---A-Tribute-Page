A Tribute to the Poets

“No matter what anybody tells you, words and ideas can change the world.” ― John Keating, Dead Poets Society

What is Dead Poets Society?

The "Dead Poets Society" was a secret club Mr. Keating was part of during his own time as a student at Welton Academy. The society's philosophy centers on embracing the beauty of poetry, encouraging free thinking, and challenging the status quo.

The students in the society express themselves creatively, and seek deeper meaning in their lives.

“Suck the marrow out of life” ― they believed.

Meet the Poets

John Keating

An English teacher known for his unconventional teaching methods and inspirational approach to education.

Keating's passionate belief in the power of poetry and literature to inspire and transform young minds challenges the traditional and rigid atmosphere of the school.

Niel Perry

A bright and ambitious young man, but he struggles under the weight of his father's expectations and the strict rules of the school.

He joins "Dead Poets Society" seeking to explore poetry and life in unconventional ways.

Todd Andersonn

A shy, introverted, and reserved young man who lacks confidence and struggles to express himself. Todd joins "Dead Poets Society," who share a love for poetry and nonconformity.

He is encouraged to overcome his fears and participate more actively in the world around him and gains the confidence to challenge authority and question the status quo.

Charlie Dalton

A free-spirited and rebellious individual with a penchant for breaking rules and challenging authority.

Charlie embraces Mr. Keating's teachings with enthusiasm and has a willingness to embrace life's opportunities and live in the moment, also encouraging his friends to do the same.

Knox Overstreet

An earnest and romantic young man who becomes infatuated with a girl named Chris, who attends a nearby girls' school.

As he joins "Dead Poets Society", Knox is encouraged to pursue his desires and not be bound by societal norms.

Gerard Pitts

A quiet yet ambitious student with poorer grades, but capable of building an illegal radio with Steven Meeks.

With a genuine interest in Mr. Keating's teachings, he joins "Dead Poets Society" to challenge the school's conservative atmosphere and to embrace the idea of living life to the fullest.

Steven Meeks

An intelligent and thoughtful young man with a keen interest in academics and science. He is known for his scientific curiosity and logical thinking.

With desire to explore unconventional ideas and challenge the traditional norms of their school, Meeks becomes an active member of the "Dead Poets Society" participating in the group's discussions and activities.

Richard Stevenson

A conformist and ambitious individual who is more concerned with adhering to authority and maintaining the status quo.

Unlike his friends who are drawn to Mr. Keating's unconventional teaching methods and emphasis on individuality, Cameron is quick to distance himself from the "Dead Poets Society" when the administration begins to investigate the group's activities.

“And medicine, law, business, engineering, these are noble pursuits and necessary to sustain life. But poetry, beauty, romance, love, these are what we stay alive for.”
― John Keating, Dead Poets Society
